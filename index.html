the founder’s letter

Made by hand.
Named with prayer.

I started wrapping stones the year I turned thirty. My faith was being reshaped by grief and grace in equal measure, and I needed something to do with my hands while I prayed. The first pieces were for myself.

Then a friend asked about the amethyst at my collarbone. I told her about Esther. She cried. I said — here. This one is yours. Every piece I make is a conversation I have had with God first. The stone finds its name in morning Scripture.

I sign each one and number it because I want you to know: someone held this before you.

— Jenny, founder & maker

what we believe

Six commitments
to every piece.

I

Named from Scripture

Every piece carries a biblical name and a verse. The name is the identity before it has a buyer.

II

Made without jigs

Wire is shaped by hand, following the stone. No two pieces are identical. This is not a flaw.

III

Numbered & retired

Each edition closes permanently. We do not reissue. When a piece is gone, it becomes memory.

IV

Ethically sourced

We purchase directly from artisan miners in Tucson, Jaipur, and Oaxaca. Fair price, always.

V

Prayed over

Before packaging, each piece is held and prayed over. Not the buyer’s name — but the intention carries.

VI

Sent slow

Sometimes we hold a piece for a day if it does not feel ready. The studio is not a warehouse.

about the studio

the maker

Jenny.
Founder, maker, keeper.

I grew up in a family that prayed at the table and kept Bibles marked with coffee rings. Faith was never abstract for us — it was domestic, daily, and very loud.

I moved to California in my mid-twenties and found that the version of faith I had brought with me needed air. I started making jewelry the way some people start journaling — without a plan, without an audience, just needing somewhere to put what I was learning.

Jenny’s Radiant Jewelry began the year I turned thirty and stopped pretending I was becoming someone else. I was already her. The studio is her name, and every piece we make is her story — told in stone and wire and copper.

“Jenny’s Radiant Jewelry — that is, Esther.”

— Esther 2:7

the studio

Where the pieces
are made and named.

The studio is a spare bedroom in a small house in California. There is a window that faces north, a table that is always covered in wire and beads, and a shelf where pieces rest while they wait for their names.

We work Monday through Friday, observe Sabbath on Sunday, and ship everything in person from the local post office. Orders placed after Thursday ship the following Monday.

There is one maker: Naomi. There are no contractors, no assistants, no fulfilment centres. This means each piece takes as long as it takes, and sometimes an order is held for a day because the piece did not feel finished yet.

We believe this is not a limitation. We believe it is what makes each piece worth wearing.

care guide

Keeping your piece
for a long time.

a note from the maker

Stone is patient.
Wire is faithful.

Each piece is made from natural stone — a material that lives, breathes, and changes with the woman who wears it. Copper wire will patina. Stones will acquire the warmth of your skin. This is not wear. This is belonging.

What follows are guidelines, not guarantees. A piece worn every day for ten years will look different from the day you received it. That difference is the story.

If you have a specific question about your piece, write to us. We will answer honestly.

By stone type.

amethyst

The Amethyst Vigil pieces

do

  • Remove before bathing or swimming
  • Store in the cotton pouch it arrived in
  • Wipe with a soft dry cloth after wearing
  • Keep away from direct sunlight when storing

avoid

  • Ultrasonic cleaners — will damage the wire wrap
  • Ammonia-based jewellery cleaners
  • Perfume or hairspray applied while wearing

jasper

The Fields of Jasper pieces

do

  • Rinse with lukewarm water if soiled
  • Pat dry immediately — never rub
  • The copper wire will patina naturally over time
  • Embrace the darkening — it adds character

avoid

  • Submerging in water for extended periods
  • Chemical jewellery polishes on the wire
  • Pulling the wire wrapping to adjust

turquoise

The Waters of Mercy pieces

do

  • Wipe with a soft slightly damp cloth
  • Store flat in the provided pouch
  • Know that turquoise can deepen in colour over years — this is prized
  • Handle by the cord, not the stone

avoid

  • Chlorine, saltwater, or hot tubs
  • Heat sources when storing
  • Dropping on hard surfaces — turquoise can crack

copper wire

All wire-wrapped pieces

do

  • Allow patina to develop naturally
  • Polish lightly with a dry cloth if you prefer a brighter finish
  • Write to us if a wrap feels loose — we can advise or repair

avoid

  • Bending or forcing the wire to adjust shape
  • Chemical dips or polishes on copper
  • Pulling the wrap away from the stone

seed beads & cord

Beaded pieces

do

  • Store loosely — never knotted or folded tightly
  • Inspect the cord annually for wear
  • Contact us for restringing if needed — we offer this service

avoid

  • Tugging at the cord to lengthen
  • Sleeping in beaded pieces
  • Getting the cord wet repeatedly

storage

All pieces

do

  • Use the embroidered cotton pouch provided
  • Store individually to prevent scratching
  • Keep in a cool, dry place away from light
  • The pouch can be washed gently by hand

avoid

  • Storing in sealed plastic (promotes tarnish)
  • Hanging turquoise pieces — the weight can stress the cord

Questions we hear often.

Can I request a custom piece?+
Custom commissions open twice a year — spring and ahead of the holiday season. Write to us with your idea and we will add you to the waitlist. We do not take every commission — only the ones that feel right.
How do I care for my piece?+
Visit our Care Guide page for stone-specific guidance. The short version: remove before swimming, store in the cotton pouch, and let copper patina naturally. Write to us if anything feels wrong with the piece.
Are your stones ethically sourced?+
Yes. We purchase directly from artisan miners at the Tucson Gem Show, the Jaipur Gem Fair, and through a small co-op in Oaxaca. We pay full asking price. We do not negotiate down.
What if my piece arrives damaged?+
Write to us within 7 days. We will arrange a replacement or a full refund — your choice. We have never had a claim we did not honour.
Do you ship internationally?+
Yes — to most countries. International orders ship via tracked airmail and typically arrive within 7–14 days. Customs duties are the responsibility of the buyer, but we declare items at their full value and label them accurately.
Can I include a gift message?+
Yes — there is a note field at checkout. Tell us who it is for and we will include it on the handwritten card alongside the piece’s verse. Gift wrapping is included at no charge for every order.
Do you offer wholesale?+
Selectively. We work with three boutiques and open one or two new partnerships per year. Use the contact form and select “wholesale or stockist inquiry.”

the process

How a custom piece
comes to be.

1

You write to us

Tell us who the piece is for, what stone comes to mind, what feeling you are hoping to give. There is no wrong answer and no required format.

2

We reply with a proposal

Within five days, Jenny will reply with a stone suggestion, a sketch in words, and a price. If it doesn’t feel right, we revise until it does.

3

A 50% deposit holds your slot

Once we’ve agreed on the design, a deposit holds your place in the queue. Custom pieces take 3–6 weeks from deposit to shipping.

4

Photos before it ships

Before we package your piece, we send photos for your approval. The remaining balance is collected at that point.

size guide

Finding your
perfect length.

Every piece is available in three cord lengths. If you’re between sizes or unsure, write to us — we can advise based on your neck circumference and preferred drape.

the three lengths

15″

Choker — high collar

Sits at the collarbone. Works well on longer necks or with open-collar shirts and dresses.

most popular
17″

Princess — mid chest

Sits just below the collarbone. The most versatile length — works with most necklines.

19″

Matinee — long drape

Falls between the chest and the waist. Wear alone or doubled. The Magnificat is designed for this length.

how to measure

Use a soft measuring tape or a piece of string held around your neck at your preferred drape point, then measure against a ruler.

If you are between lengths or have a specific request, choose the longer size and write a note at checkout. We can adjust before shipping.

not sure?

Write to us with your neck measurement and we will recommend the right length. We have never had a return for sizing when someone has asked first.
